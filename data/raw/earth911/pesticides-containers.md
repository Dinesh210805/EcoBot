# How to Recycle Pesticides & Containers
![](https://earthnew.wpenginepowered.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Pesticides-header.jpg)
Pesticide liquids and granules cannot be recycled. But pesticide containers can be recycled, and these containers also need to undergo special treatment and cannot simply be tossed into your curbside bin.
[Find Recycling Guides for Other Materials](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<https:/earth911.com/recycling-center-search-guides/> "Recycling Center Search & Recycling Guides")
### Frequent Pesticide & Container Recycling Questions
#### Can liquid or granulated pesticide be disposed of by pouring it down a drain or dumping it outside?
No, pesticides should never be disposed of in nature, poured down a drain, or placed in the trash. Take them to a local hazardous waste facility — [find locations near you](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<https:/search.earth911.com/?what=Pesticides&where=zip+code&list_filter=all&max_distance=25&family_id=&latitude=&longitude=&country=&province=&city=&sponsor=>).
#### Can empty plastic pesticide containers be reused or recycled?
Pesticide containers should not be reused, [according to the EPA](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<https:/www.epa.gov/safepestcontrol/safe-disposal-pesticides>). Pesticide containers may be able to be recycled, depending on location and specifications of local recyclers and the material designation listed on the container.
#### Do pesticides expire?
Yes. Most pesticides have an indicated shelf-life of at least two years from the time of manufacture, [according to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<http:/www.fao.org/docrep/V8966E/V8966e02.htm> "FAO Pesticide shelf-life"), but may still be usable long after the indicated shelf-life has expired.
#### How are pesticide containers recycled?
Depending on state and local regulations, containers are generally triple-rinsed, then either processed or shipped to a facility that is equipped to handle the plastic of which the container is composed. Containers can be recycled into nearly any type of plastic product.
