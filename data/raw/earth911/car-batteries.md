# How to Recycle Car Batteries
![](https://earthnew.wpenginepowered.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/car-batteries-header.jpg)
Many, many people and companies recycle car batteries. In fact, car batteries are the most recycled products in the U.S., having a 98 percent to 99 percent recycling rate. Most individuals simply return their old car batteries to the dealership or the store where they are purchasing their replacement.

### Frequent Car Batteries Recycling Questions
#### Can one get money for an old car battery?
Yes. Car batteries can be scrapped at most scrap yards, [according to iScrap App](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<http:/iscrapapp.blogspot.com/> "The iScrap App"). Some auto parts retailers also take a deposit on a new battery when it is purchased, so you may be entitled for a pay-out when you return it to that retailer for recycling when it’s spent.
#### What do auto parts stores and service shops do with old batteries?
In accordance with [various state and regional laws](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<http:/batterycouncil.org/resource/resmgr/Docs/BCIMODEL.pdf> "State and Local Laws") and contracts with battery manufacturers and recyclers, certified service shops ship out batteries for recycling en masse. More than [98 percent of battery lead](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<http:/batterycouncil.org/?page=Battery_Recycling> "Battery lead") is recycled, according to Battery Council International.
#### Are there any recycled materials in car batteries?
Yes. The typical new lead-acid battery contains 60 to 80 percent recycled lead and plastic, [according to Battery Council International](https://earth911.com/recycling-guide/<http:/batterycouncil.org/?page=Battery_Recycling> "The Battery Council International").
#### Do I need to pay to recycle an old car battery?
Most auto parts stores offer complimentary battery recycling with the purchase of a qualifying new car battery.
